Mainstay Tech · Field Manual

Mainstay Forge

Our in-house clip factory. Bring in a song, a video, or a long interview and Forge hands you finished, branded, vertical clips — several versions at once, ready to post. You always hit send yourself; nothing posts on its own.

Open it atforge.groundrushcloud.com
01

Signing in

You each have your own login now — Forge records who made every clip.

  • Go to forge.groundrushcloud.com on your phone or computer.
  • Enter your username and password (sent to you privately).
  • Top-right shows who you're signed in as. Your work appears in the Queue under your name.
  • Need a login added or changed? That's on Mike — just ask him.
02

What's where

Each tab does one job. Left side on desktop, bottom bar on your phone.

  • Create — build a post from scratch: a leak graphic, a kinetic lyric, or a film montage.
  • Topic — bring in a long video/podcast/interview and cut the exact moments out of it. The smart one.
  • Queue — watch what's being made, and who made it.
  • Library — everything Forge has finished: preview, download, delete.
  • Distribute — line clips up and push them to Postiz as drafts.
  • Intel — the performance scoreboard. Not switched on yet — Mike's still setting it up.
03

Create — build from scratch Live

Pick a style, fill the boxes, hit “Forge it.” It lands in the Queue, then the Library.

A · Leak Graphic
A “leaked” album graphic. Type the Title, a Tracklist (one song per line), and a Caption. New: you can now upload your own graphic and Forge bakes the title + tracklist + LEAKED tag right over it. Leave the upload empty and it makes the artwork for you with AI.
B · Kinetic Lyric
A lyric video synced to a hook. Upload the audio hook and type the lyric. You get a vertical video with the words popping in time to the song.
C · Film Montage
A fast-cut montage. List your clip sources one per line (a YouTube/TikTok/Instagram link, or search: rainy night city) and upload the song hook. You get a rhythmic montage cut to the song — it auto-picks ~2.5-second beats across your clips, and it runs exactly as long as the hook you give it.

Shared options

  • Caption font — the lettering style.
  • Deliver to — which folder it saves into.
  • Remix looks — how many genuinely different-looking versions to make.
  • Variations — near-identical copies of each look, so the same post can go to several accounts without flagging as a duplicate.
04

Topic — cut clips out of a long video Live

“Find the good moments and cut them for me.” This is the heart of Forge.

1
Add a source — by link OR by file. At the top of Topic there's an Add a source box. Paste a YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram link and hit Fetch & transcribe — Forge downloads it and reads it. Or, for a big file you already have, open the drop folder, drag it in, and hit Refresh. Either way it shows up in your Source list.
2
Search a topic. Pick the source, type what you want in plain words — “the part where he talks about growing up” — and hit Find segments. It lists the matching moments with timestamps and the exact words.
3
Pick & trim. Tick the moments you want; nudge the start/end to tighten them.
4
Set the caption look. Style: Clean (a line at a time), Karaoke (each word lights up as he talks), or Bold caps. Plus position (lower/center/upper), font (Anton, Bebas, Archivo, Bungee, Black Ops, DM Sans), and color (the highlight color for karaoke). Captions follow his real words automatically.
5
Assemble variants. Set how many and hit “Assemble variants” — Forge makes several different branded, captioned cuts of your picked moments.

Montage tray — stitch moments across clips

Want specific moments from more than one source in one clip?

  • Search a source, pick moments, hit Add selection. Switch sources, pick more, add again.
  • The tray lists every pick — drag to reorder, or remove any you don't want.
  • Song bed (optional): add a song to play under it. Under voice (duck) keeps the talking up front and tucks the music low underneath; Replace audio is music-only.
  • Hit Assemble montage — it stitches your exact picks, in your order.
05

Where your clips go

Every job follows the same path — so you always know where to find your work.

You hit Forge it / Assemble→ Queue (it's rendering)→ Library (finished — preview & download)→ Distribute (push to Postiz as drafts)
  • Everything is saved to our shared Mainstay cloud automatically — nothing lives only on your phone.
  • In Distribute, Forge lines each variant up to a different account and pushes them to Postiz as drafts. A human still hits send — Forge never posts on its own.
  • Made something you don't want? Delete it from the Library (it goes to a trash you can undo).
06

Getting creative

The tool's only as good as the angles you bring it. A few ways to make it sing:

  • Mix sources in one montage — a bar from an interview, a moment from a live clip, a line from a podcast, stitched in your order.
  • Try the caption styles — Karaoke pops on TikTok; Bold caps reads loud on mute; Clean is the safe, classy default. Same clip, three feels.
  • Use a song bed under talking — drop a Rod Wave instrumental under an interview cut and let it duck under his voice. Instant mood.
  • Upload your own leak graphic — bring real artwork and let Forge bake the tracklist on it.
  • Make a few looks, not fifty — pick the 2–3 that actually hit and push those wide with Variations.
—

Be thoughtful — don't burn it up

Every clip Forge makes uses real compute and AI credits behind the scenes. It's powerful, so it's easy to spam — please don't. Before you assemble, think about what you're actually making and why.

  • Make deliberate clips — pick your moments, then assemble. Don't fire off dozens of throwaways to “see what happens.”
  • A handful of strong variants beats a hundred mediocre ones — and keeps the Library clean for everyone.
  • Re-cut when something's off (caption style, trim) instead of regenerating from scratch over and over.
  • Treat it like studio time, not a slot machine.

The rules Forge always follows

  • Every clip is vertical 9:16 and between 10 and 60 seconds.
  • Everything comes out branded in the Mainstay style.
  • Nothing posts itself — it only ever makes drafts.
  • YouTube / TikTok / Instagram are used to pull clips from, never to post to.

Hit a snag? Reach out to Mike

If something breaks, looks wrong, won't load, or you're just not sure — reach out to Mike. Don't fight it or work around it. A few quick notes:

  • A link won't load — it's probably age-locked or private; download it and use the drop folder.
  • A wrong word in a caption — that's the transcript guessing slang; flag it.
  • Anything else — message Mike and it gets handled.
